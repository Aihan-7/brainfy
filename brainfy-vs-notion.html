Brainfy vs Notion for Studying

Notion is a brilliant place to organise notes. But organising isn’t remembering. Brainfy is purpose-built for the part Notion leaves to you: turning notes into recall with spaced repetition, AI flashcards, and focus tools.

Open Brainfy → All study resources

Different jobs

Notion is a flexible workspace — databases, docs, wikis. That flexibility is its strength and, for studying, its cost: you end up building a study system instead of studying. Brainfy is opinionated about one outcome — you remember the material — and ships the whole loop to get there.

Use them together

Keep your long-form notes and project docs in Notion. When it’s time to actually memorise, paste a section into Brainfy, generate a deck, and let spaced repetition do the rest. Notion holds the knowledge; Brainfy gets it into your head.

Frequently asked questions

Can Notion do spaced repetition?

Only through DIY templates or third-party widgets — there’s no native scheduler. Brainfy has spaced repetition built in.

Should I replace Notion with Brainfy?

Not necessarily — they do different jobs. Notion organises; Brainfy memorises. Many students keep notes in Notion and review in Brainfy.

Can I move notes from Notion into Brainfy?

Yes — paste a note or export text, and the AI drafts flashcards from it; or paste a term/definition list to import directly.

Is Brainfy free?

Yes, free during beta.
